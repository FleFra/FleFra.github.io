The Filth

A Three-Day Game Jam Project

About The Filth

"The Filth" is a Unity game developed over the course of three days during a game jam in a small team. The game mostly focuses on aesthetics and simple, addicting gameplay. Clean your desktop by clearing icons while the virus "The Filth" slowly gets stronger.

Controls

  • Press ~ to pause the game
  • Move your mouse to move
  • Hold left mouse button to attack
  • Right click to fire your special, right click again to activate it
  • Press tab to show or hide controls

All of the sprites in this game were self-made and inspired by Windows 95 and 98 icons. The sound design mostly consists of existing Windows sounds.

My Contribution

I played a key role in bringing "The Filth" to life during our game jam. My contributions included:

  • Game Design: Making sure all of the key-mechanics worked properly for a smooth gameplay experience.
  • Art and Visuals: Created all in-game sprites inspired by retro Windows 95 and 98 icons, giving the game its unique aesthetic.
  • Programming: Developed core mechanics, mainly focussing on player movement.
  • Sound Integration: Selected and integrated Windows sounds into the game to enhance the nostalgic feel.
  • Team Collaboration: Worked closely with teammates to brainstorm ideas, solve challenges, and meet the strict three-day deadline.

This game-jam was a great way to experience what it was like to work with a strict deadline. It was a good way to learn how to work together as efficiently as possible to finish the game before the deadline.
